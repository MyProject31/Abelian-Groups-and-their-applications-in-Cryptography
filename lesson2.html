Definition (Direct Product of Groups)

Let G₁ and G₂ be two groups with operations *₁ and *₂ respectively. The direct product of G₁ and G₂ is the set:

G₁ × G₂ = { (g₁, g₂) | g₁ ∈ G₁, g₂ ∈ G₂ }

with the operation defined component-wise:

(g₁, g₂) · (h₁, h₂) = (g₁ *₁ h₁, g₂ *₂ h₂)

Properties

  • If G₁ and G₂ are abelian, then G₁ × G₂ is also abelian.
  • The identity element is (e₁, e₂), where e₁ and e₂ are the identities of G₁ and G₂.
  • The inverse of (g₁, g₂) is (g₁⁻¹, g₂⁻¹).
  • The order of the direct product is |G₁ × G₂| = |G₁| · |G₂|.

Example

ℤ₂ × ℤ₃ = { (0,0), (0,1), (0,2), (1,0), (1,1), (1,2) }

Addition is modulo 2 in the first component and modulo 3 in the second.

Fundamental Theorem of Finite Abelian Groups

Theorem

Let G be a finite abelian group of order n. Then G can be expressed uniquely as a direct product of cyclic groups of prime-power order.

Primary Decomposition

Let G be a finite abelian group. Then G can be expressed as a direct product of cyclic groups whose orders are powers of prime numbers:

G ≅ ℤ_{p₁^{k₁}} × ℤ_{p₂^{k₂}} × ... × ℤ_{pᵣ^{kᵣ}}

where p₁, p₂, …, pᵣ are primes and k₁, k₂, …, kᵣ are positive integers.

Invariant Factor Decomposition

G can also be expressed as:

G ≅ ℤ_{n₁} × ℤ_{n₂} × ... × ℤ_{nᵣ}

where n₁, n₂, …, nᵣ are positive integers such that:

n₁ | n₂ | ... | nᵣ

Examples of Decomposition

Group of order 12: |G| = 12 = 2² · 3

Primary Decomposition: G ≅ ℤ₄ × ℤ₃

Invariant Factor Decomposition: G ≅ ℤ₂ × ℤ₆

Group of order 8: |G| = 8 = 2³

Primary Decomposition: ℤ₈, ℤ₄ × ℤ₂, ℤ₂ × ℤ₂ × ℤ₂

Invariant Factor Decomposition: ℤ₈, ℤ₂ × ℤ₄, ℤ₂ × ℤ₂ × ℤ₂
